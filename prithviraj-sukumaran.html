Some Lesser Known Facts About Prithviraj Sukumaran

  • Prithviraj is the son of late Malayalam actor Sukumaran and Malayalam actress Mallika Sukumaran.
  • He started his acting career in 2002 by playing the role of Manu Nandakumar in the Malayalam film Nandanam.
  • He is the only person to date, who has won the title of Mr. LA Fest twice in the Annual Inter-School arts festival hosted by Loyola School, Thiruvananthapuram.
  • He has worked in many languages such as Malayalam, Tamil, Telugu, and Hindi.
  • Apart from being an actor, he is also a playback singer and has sung numerous Malayalam songs like Kaane Kaane, Kaattu Paranjathum, Kettille Kettille, Njan, etc.
  • In 2010, he became the brand ambassador of hi-tech ambulance services 108, which was launched by the National Rural Health Mission. He also represented Kalyan Silks.
  • In 2011, he won National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Malayalam (Producer) and Kerala State Film Award for Best Film (Producer) for the Malayalam film Indian Rupee.
  • He also won Kerala State Film Awards for Best Actor for the Malayalam films Vaasthavam (2006), Celluloid (2013), and Ayalum Njanum Thammil (2012).
  • In 2013, he won Filmfare Critics Award for Best Actor – South for the Malayalam film Celluloid.
  • He runs a restaurant named Spice Boat in Doha, Qatar along with his brother and mother.
  • During his engineering time, he had a crush on a girl but he was shocked when he came to know that she was a lesbian.
  • He is the youngest actor who broke the record of Mohanlal (Actor) by receiving the Kerala State Film Award for Best Actor, which was held by Mohanlal for about 20 years.
